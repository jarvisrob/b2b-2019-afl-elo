A data scientist does footy tipping¶

Rob Jarvis¶

Back to Base, Friday 7 June 2019¶

In 1960, the United States Chess Federation implemented a new system for rating chess players, which was devised by the physics professor, Arpad Elo. Professor Elo would go on to publish his work in the book, The Rating of Chessplayers, Past and Present.

Today, we can use modified versions of Professor Elo’s work for its ultimate purpose: to predict AFL games. To be honest, if there’s a better use for statistics and probability than to predict the chance that the Hawks will win the flag, I don’t want to hear about it.

Come along to hear how I use bell curves, parameter optimisation and then throw in some regression to the mean to answer the big questions. Just be aware that the big questions are along the lines of “Should I tip the Swans or the Pies in tonight’s game at the SCG?”. the Tigers or the Cats in tonight's game at the MCG?".

normal_pdf_curve <- function(mu, sigma, n_pts) {
    n_sigmas <- 4
    x <- seq(from = mu - 4 * sigma, to = mu + 4 * sigma, length.out = n_pts)
    pdf <- dnorm(x, mean = mu, sd = sigma)
    curve <- tibble(x, pdf)
}

travel_curve <- function(c1, c2, n_pts) {
    d <- seq(from = 0, to = 4000, length.out = n_pts)
    T <- c1 * (d ^ c2)
    curve <- tibble(d, T)
}

If the rating scale and the uncertainty (standard deviation) in performance are arbitrary, then what matters is the ratings you give to players¶

So how do you rate players?¶

The answer is iteratively: Keep updating based on new information¶

  • Adjust a player's rating after each game, either up or down
  • Adjustment is based on their performance relative to expectations
  • Expectation is equal to the probabilty that a player will win
$$R_A'=R_A+K(S_A-E_A)$$

where

  • $R_A'=$ new rating for Player A
  • $R_A=$ old rating for Player A
  • $K=$ adjustment coefficient
  • $S_A=$ score, being 1 if Player A wins, 0 if they lose, 0.5 if they draw
  • $E_A=$ expectation, the probability of Player A winning

Note that when Player A vs Player B:

$$S_B=1-S_A$$

and

$$E_B=1-E_A$$

which means that

$$R_B'=R_B+K(S_B-E_B)=R_B-K(S_A-E_A)$$

In effect, points are transferred from the player that underperformed expectations to the player that overperformed expectations. The amount transferred is proportional to the difference between the actual result and the expected result:

$$K(S_A-E_A)$$

The Elo rating algorithm is continually tuning itself based on the last result¶

Run through an example, use $K=20$

The big question is then ... What is the correct value of $K$?¶

  • A big value of $K$ means ratings change rapidly, and more closely reflect recent form
  • A small value of $K$ means ratings change slowly, and more closely align with talent

Tuning $K$ is key to making Elo's rating system work¶

Our scale and the uncertainty (standard deviation) are no longer arbitrary--they are linked to the predicted margin and the uncertainty in the margin¶

But it's circular:

  • Need to know $\sigma$ to predict the margin
  • Need to know the predicted margin to calculate $\sigma$

That's the basics of a Elo rating system for AFL, but we can do more¶

Regression to the mean between seasons¶

  • Biggest unknowns tend to be at the start of a season
  • All teams are regressed to the mean before the start of a new season
  • Regression amount is controlled by the distance from the mean rating, i.e. $0$, and parameter $\alpha$
$$R_A' = R_A - \alpha(R_A-0)=(1-\alpha)R_A$$

Ground ratings for each team¶

  • For each team on each ground
  • Iterative adjustment of team performance on that ground

Update after each game by amount:

$$G(S_A-E_A)$$

It's hard to play on the road because Australia is big¶

$$T=C_1d^{C_2}$$

where

  • $T=$ travel penalty
  • $d=$ distance traveled
  • $C_1=$ travel penalty coefficient, with $C_1\geq0$
  • $C_2=$ travel penalty exponent, with $0{\leq}C_2{\leq}1$

It becomes an optimisation problem¶

Need to tune the values of seven parameters:

  • $\sigma$ to match the observed uncertainty in predicting margins
  • $K=$ the coefficient for updating ratings
  • $\alpha=$ the regression fraction between seasons
  • $G=$ the coefficient for ground rating updates
  • $C_1$ and $C_2$, the travel penalty parameters
  • $R_{expn,0}=$ the initial rating for expansion teams

with the goal of making the probabilities and margins accurate, and the margin as precise as possible
